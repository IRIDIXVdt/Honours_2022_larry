Purpose:

You are being invited to participate in this research study for an undergraduate Honours Thesis project to assess the effectiveness of spaced repetition algorithm in learning MIPS in COSC211. Responses collected from participants will provide insights on the teaching methods used in this course. Responses will be analyzed in order to identify strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats related to the use of customizable learning material in this course, and hence help the instructor (i.e., the principal investigator) improve future offerings of this course as well as other courses that use the same material and teaching technique. 

Study Procedures:

Barbara Komlos, a consultant from the Center of Teaching and Learning (CTL) at UBC-O, has explained this study to you and answered questions. No grades or other incentives will be interfering with this research process. If you want to participate in the study, you will be completing a list of flashcard questions on a website provided by the study team every day. It will take approximately one hour per week to complete the tasks which are designed to help you study MIPS. You can access the website anytime in Term2. 

You need to use an email and password for registration and login. You need to accept a verification link which will be sent to your email before you can login. Please use an email with no reference to your real identity so that data collection is completely anonymous. The online portal will not collect any data whatsoever about your identities (including names, IDs, or IP addresses). If you consent to provide performance data, your responses cannot be linked back to you.

The collected data will be stored for 5 years so that it can be compared to future similar studies. As indicated above, an undergraduate Honours Thesis student is part of the study team; he helped with preparing the questionnaire and will help analyze the collected data. 
 

Study Results:

Your performance data will be used for the purpose of research analysis. All students in the class will be given an opportunity to participate in the study by registering and completing tasks on the online portal. If you do not provide consent, you can still access the website, and no performance data will be collected.

The main study findings may be published in conference proceedings or journal articles. 

Potential Risks:

We do not believe there are any potential risks to participants. 

  • Your participation in the study is voluntarily and will not affect your course experience in any way. 
  • No grades or other incentives will be interfering with this research process. In other words, you will not be penalized or rewarded for your participation.
  • No identifiable data will be collected. The study team will collect the anonymous user performance data on the website. All data on the server will be removed after downloading them and be stored securely by the study team.
  • While the instructor will have access to the performance data, he will neither know who consent nor know who is accessing the portal. He will not be able to link the data back to you. 
  • Barbara Komlos from the Center of Teaching and Learning (CTL) will be overseeing the study team to make sure the data collection of the survey is done on UBC Qualtrics. The study team will set up the online survey so that it does not collect any identifiable data. After data is downloaded from the Qualtrics server, it will be removed from there stored securely by the study team. You can still access the website even if you do not consent to provide performance data. To withdraw your consent, go to account page and click on consent information section. Once you withdraw your consent, all collections of your performance data will be removed.

Potential Benefits:

Your performance will be used to refine and improve the web portal to be used in this course and similar ones. This might be helpful for you and others who may use similar online portal in the future.

Conflict of Interest:

Due to the dual role as both an instructor and a researcher, the PI will only have access to anonymized data after the course appeal data has passed, and he will not know who participate and/or consent to provide data in the study. In his role as an instructor, he wants to improve the system so that it can be used for future course offerings. This does not conflict with his researcher's role as analyzing students’ feedback, whether positive or negative, can help improve the system. 
 
Confidentiality:

Anonymized performance data will be store at Firestore Database from https://firebase.google.com/. Only the team members listed above will have access to the anonymized data. Collected data will be securely stored on UBC password-protected laptop for 5 years following any publication(s) and then securely destroyed.
